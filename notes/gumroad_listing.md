## Description (full body — Markdown)

> *Some quasicrystals fold under nonlinear stress. Others hold. The difference is structural — and it has a name.*
>
> **Chapter η** is a 20-page reader's companion to *Differential Nonlinear Robustness of Critical States in Fibonacci and Tribonacci Substitution Chains* (Nogueira Grossi, 2026). It walks through the result, the math behind it, and the Lean 4 verification, in plain language with annotated derivations.
>
> The result, in one paragraph: take a 1D quasiperiodic chain — the Fibonacci chain, generated by `A → AB`, `B → A` — pluck a critical mid-gap eigenstate, and turn on a nonlinear interaction (DNLS, λ ≈ 1.5). The state delocalizes hard: 57% loss of localization. Take the same critical state on the **tribonacci** chain — generated by `A → AB`, `B → AC`, `C → A` — and the same nonlinear kick barely registers: under 5% loss. The tribonacci chain's stronger multifractality buys real resistance. The Perron–Frobenius eigenvalue η ≈ 1.839287 of the tribonacci companion matrix is the structural fingerprint, formally verified in Lean 4/Mathlib4 without `sorry`.
>
> ## What's inside
>
> 1. **Annotated PDF** (~20 pages, *Principia Orthogona* house style) — the full result walked through: substitution rules, mid-gap eigenstates, the IPR collapse, the Lean proof in plain English, parameter tables, and open questions. Reader's-guide voice, not a reprint.
> 2. **Jupyter notebook** (`.ipynb`) — runnable, reproduces figures 1–5 from scratch, with markdown narrative between cells. Walks through the IPR calculation step by step. Drop-in for teaching.
> 3. **Annotated Lean 4 file** (`.lean`) — the `TribonacciDNLS.lean` theorem set with inline comments explaining each step to a non-Lean reader. Educational, not a stand-alone proof bundle.
